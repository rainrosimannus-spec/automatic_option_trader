# Lawyer Review Checklist — Loan Agreement Template v1

Before the first external lender uses a Bruno-generated agreement, an Estonian
corporate lawyer should review and confirm or correct each of the following:

## High-priority review items

1. **Section 7 (Events of Default)** — cure periods, default thresholds, and
   the definition of "material" must align with Estonian Bankruptcy Act and
   Reorganisation Act. Confirm 15-day cure is enforceable and standard.

2. **Section 8 (Acceleration and Remedies)** — verify acceleration mechanics
   are compatible with Estonian Law of Obligations Act (Võlaõigusseadus).
   Confirm default interest rate (+5%) is enforceable and not usurious.

3. **Section 11 (Subordination Acknowledgment)** — verify that the
   subordination language is enforceable against shareholder lenders in
   Estonian insolvency. Decide whether separate subordination agreements
   with each shareholder lender are required (likely yes) or whether this
   acknowledgment alone is sufficient (likely no).

4. **Section 12 (AML / Source of Funds)** — confirm language matches
   Estonian Money Laundering and Terrorist Financing Prevention Act
   (Rahapesu ja terrorismi rahastamise tõkestamise seadus). Verify
   beneficial ownership threshold (currently set at 25%) and record-keeping
   period (currently 5 years).

5. **Section 13 (Governing Law / Jurisdiction)** — confirm Tallinn District
   Court (Harju Maakohus) is the appropriate forum. Consider whether the
   arbitration alternative needs more specific drafting.

## Medium-priority review items

6. **Section 9 (Covenants)** — confirm covenant definitions are clear and
   enforceable. The "net worth" covenant ties to Bruno's headroom math —
   ensure the definition matches Bruno's actual calculation.

7. **Section 6 (Representations and Warranties)** — verify language is
   complete and that breach triggers in Section 7 link correctly.

8. **Section 15.7 (Confidentiality)** — confirm scope is appropriate for
   Estonian commercial practice.

## Translation review

9. **Estonian translation** — verify that the controlling Estonian version
   matches the English in substance, and that the controlling-language clause
   (Section 15.6) is enforceable.

## Per-lender additions

The lawyer may add the following per-lender, depending on the lender's profile:

- Specific warranties from the lender (e.g., for entity lenders with complex
  ownership: warranties about authority and corporate approvals)
- Specific notice provisions (e.g., for non-Estonian-resident lenders)
- Specific source-of-funds documentation requirements based on jurisdiction
- Additional reporting obligations if the lender has unusual transparency
  requirements

## What NOT to include without explicit lender request

- Performance kickers or profit-sharing — would jeopardize the non-AIF posture
- Convertibility into equity — would jeopardize the non-AIF posture and
  trigger securities law considerations
- Pre-payment penalties — included only if specifically negotiated and at a
  modest level
- Anti-dilution provisions — N/A for pure debt, would imply quasi-equity
- Voting or governance rights — would change the legal character of the loan
